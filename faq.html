Olympics FAQ

A: The first modern Olympic Games were held in Athens, Greece, in 1896.

A: The Summer Olympic Games are held every four years, while the Winter Olympic Games are also held every four years but alternating with the Summer Games, resulting in an Olympic event every two years.

A: Host cities for the Olympic Games are selected through a bidding process where cities submit proposals to the International Olympic Committee (IOC). The IOC evaluates the proposals and selects the host city through a voting process.

A: The five Olympic rings represent the five continents of the world (Africa, America, Asia, Europe, and Oceania) and are a symbol of the unity and friendship among athletes from different parts of the globe.

A: The Olympic motto is "Citius, Altius, Fortius," which is Latin for "Faster, Higher, Stronger."
